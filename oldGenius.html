    The Weeknd

    “The Hills” is the fifth track from The Weeknd’s album Beauty Behind The Madness. Originally nicknamed “Mood Music” and teased in a live performance at SXSW, this song was finally released on May 27, 2015 with a new title — “The Hills.” The song’s narrative is about a sinking sexual affair in which Abel sets the record straight about what this woman means to him, and reminding her that she is an equally guilty party in this affair.

  • Trap Queen
    Fetty Wap

    “Trap Queen” is a gratifying tale of Fetty Wap and the three things he loves the most: his money, the bando, and his girlfriend, the Trap Queen. A few months after releasing this song, the Paterson, New Jersey native signed to Lyor Cohen’s 300 Entertainment. Nearly a year after it was released, the song became a crossover sensation, hitting the #2 spot on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. Fetty spoke to Complex about his inspiration for the song: I was just dealing with somebody at the time, and she was holding me down. We were building a lot, and I came up with the concept. She was my trap queen. Click the play button in the header to view the official music video

  • Back to Back
    Drake

    Drake releases a second track aimed at Meek Mill on July 29th, just four days after releasing “Charged Up.” The title, “Back To Back,” refers to this song as his second diss track in the past week, as well as winning back to back championships. The release date of this track was no mistake, as the Phillies and Blue Jays faced off that same night (July 29th, 2015) on Sportsnet. Poetically, the Blue Jays defeated the Phillies, 8-2. The song cover on Soundcloud shows Joe Carter, of Drake’s hometown team the Toronto Blue Jays, after he hit the home run that won the 1993 World Series against Meek’s hometown team, the Philadelphia Phillies. The Blue Jays won the year before in 1992, earning “back to back” titles. Carter went to twitter to personally thank Drake for using his picture as cover art.

  • Can't Feel My Face
    The Weeknd

    “Can’t Feel My Face” is the third single from The Weeknd’s second studio album; it leaked along with “In The Night” and “Girls Born in the 90s,” on May 25, 2015. The song later premiered live at Apple’s WWDC, in conjunction with the announcement of the Apple Music service. This song personifies drug addiction, likening it to the love of a woman and has been described as “classic pop” by critics, comparing its sound to that of Michael Jackson.
